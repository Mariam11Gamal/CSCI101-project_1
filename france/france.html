Location Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower was built by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, which was to celebrate the 100th year anniversary of the French Revolution. Its construction in 2 years, 2 months and 5 days was a veritable technical and architectural achievement..

Location The Palace of Versailles

Since 1979, the Palace of Versailles has been listed as a WorldHeritage and is one of the greatest achievements in French 17thcentury art. Louis XIII's old hunting pavilion wastransformed and extended by his son, Louis XIV, when heinstalled the Court and government there in 1682

Location Louvre Museums

The Louvre Museum in Paris, France, is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument, renowned for its vast collection of art and artifacts, including the famous "Mona Lisa" and "Venus de Milo, it opened as a public museum in 1793. Its iconic glass pyramid entrance blends classical and modern design, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Location Mont Saint-Michel (Normandy)

Mont Saint-Michel is a historic island commune in Normandy, France, renowned for its stunning medieval abbey perched atop a rocky hill. Surrounded by shifting tides, it becomes inaccessible at high tide, adding to its mystique. This UNESCO World Heritage site attracts visitors for its unique architecture and breathtaking views.
