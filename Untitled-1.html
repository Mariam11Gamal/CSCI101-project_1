The Kremlin and Red Square

The Kremlin is the historic and political heart of Moscow, containing government buildings, ancient churches, and museums. Next to it, Red Square is home to the iconic Saint Basil’s Cathedral, famous for its colorful onion domes. This area is one of the most important symbols of Russian heritage and culture.

Lake Baikal - Siberia

Lake Baikal is the world’s oldest and deepest freshwater lake, known for its stunning natural beauty. Surrounded by mountains, it transforms in winter into a frozen wonderland, with crystal-clear ice formations that attract visitors. Activities like ice-skating, dog sledding, and hiking make Baikal a must-visit for nature lovers.

St.Petersburg and Peterhof Palace

Known for its historical and architectural beauty, St. Petersburg is home to the famous Peterhof Palace, also known as the "Russian Versailles." The palace features grand gardens and intricate fountains, along with the Hermitage Museum, which houses one of the largest collections of art and artifacts in the world.

Kul Sharif Mosque brief

The Kul Sharif Mosque in Kazan is a modern symbol of Tatar heritage within the Kazan Kremlin. Originally destroyed in 1552, it was rebuilt in 2005 with striking blue domes and tall minarets, combining Islamic and Tatar architectural styles. It serves as both a place of worship and a cultural museum, celebrating Tatar history and diversity.
