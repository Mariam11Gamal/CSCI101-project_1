Location Sydney Harbour Bridge

The Sydney Harbour Bridge, completed in 1932 during the Great Depression, symbolizes Australian resilience and innovation. Designed by John Bradfield, it connected Sydney’s shores, provided jobs, and became a symbol of unity and progress in celebrations

Location Uluru

Uluru, or Ayers Rock, is a sacred sandstone monolith in Australia’s Northern Territory, significant to the Indigenous Anangu people. With ancient rock art and sacred stories, it symbolizes their deep cultural heritage and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Location Port Arthur

Port Arthur, established in 1833 in Tasmania, was a notorious convict settlement known for its harsh conditions. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it serves as a key historical landmark, reflecting Australia’s colonial past and the impact of the convict system.

Location Old Melbourne Gaol

Old Melbourne Gaol, built in the mid-1800s, is a historic prison known for housing infamous criminals like Ned Kelly. Now a museum, it highlights early prison life and Australia’s penal history, serving as a cultural landmark of colonial law and order.
